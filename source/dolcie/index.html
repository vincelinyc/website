What to expect

The on-site is an opportunity to meet face to face with everyone on the team and for you to show us your work. We’ve put together an overview of the process so that you’re prepared and know what to expect. While we try to stick to the script that is outlined below, sometimes we veer off to dig into interesting work experiences, or talk about your interests. Don’t worry if this happens, we regard spontaneity as a good thing.

Dresscode

Our work environment is pretty casual. Please wear whatever clothes you feel most comfortable in. We will probably be wearing something like jeans and a t-shirt or a funky dress.

The 1:1 interviews

The 1:1 interview is a chance for us to learn more about your personality, experience and future goals. The discussion won’t be extremely scripted, but we probably will ask many of the questions below.

Background

Who are you? What is your background? How did you get into design? Where have you worked?

Current role

What are you working on these days? What is/was your previous position? What role have you played? What type of role are looking to move into?

Impact

What is a high impact contribution that you’ve made at a previous job? What is a design improvement that you’ve driven or led? How did this lead to a better team or a better product outcome? How do you balance user needs with business needs?

Process

If you could run your perfect product design process, what would it look like? Who would be involved? What type of environment has led to your most successful design work?

Decision making

How have you failed and what did you learn? When did you change your mind based on the input of someone else?

The role

We’re sure you have some questions for us so at the end of the 1:1 we’ll give you the opportunity to ask about our design team, how we work, what we’re building at the moment, and anything else you’re curious about.

Portfolio review

The portfolio presentation is a chance for us to get to know your process and your design accomplishments. You will have one hour to talk about one or two projects that you’ve worked on. An ideal project is one that best illustrates your entire design capability and process. The presentation can be as informal or as formal as you would like, but be prepared for a lot of questions throughout related to the points below.

Communication skills

Can you clearly articulate your ideas? Can you make a compelling argument for why you made certain design decisions? Are you able to distill complex problems into easy-to-understand narratives? Are you able to process and internalize feedback?

Focus on outcomes

Can you clearly identify the success criteria for your projects? Can you explain how you executed against that criteria and what the outcome was?

Design experience

Are you familiar with the entire design process? How did you perform your research and why? Are your product outcomes thoughtful and clear? Do you design with interaction and state in mind?

Tenacity

Do you drive projects forward? Do you look for help when stuck? Do you enable and motivate your colleagues working around you?

App critique

This is for us to get a better understanding of your product thinking by considering the purpose of the app and strategic decisions that could be behind the design. During these 45 minutes you will talk with Ashwini and Vince about a product you use a lot. This is by all means a conversation and you won’t have to prepare a presentation. Although please prepare the following:

3 options

Pick 3 well known (think Google Maps) software products you would want to discuss and send them to us at least 3 days before your interview so we can review. We’ll pick one the day of your interview.

Tools

Make sure that in each case the software is the product. An e-commerce app like Amazon Shopping for example would not qualify since that’s not the kind of product work you’d be doing at Q.

Example questions

What's the purpose of this app? Why use this rather than a competitor? Is it monetized and if so, How? What does the app do well? What needs improvement? What would you change, and why? Why do you think it's designed the way it is? Is there anything you'd do differently? If you were the Head of Product, what would you have your team focus on next?
